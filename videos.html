Winds from Massive Stars

Below is a link to videos of MHD simulations for the O star Zeta Pup. Zeta Pup itself is a non-magnetic star, however for the purpose of these simulations it has been given a dipole magnetic field with an equatorial field strength of 300 G. Zeta Pup has a radius and mass which are 14 and 40 times respectively, that of the sun.

There are four videos in this collection. All are conducted with ideal MHD and the isothermal equation of state, except for one video which is ideal and adiabatic (the name of the video states that it is adiabatic). The units of density in the videos are g/cm^3 and velocity cm/s. The unit of time (given in the top left) in the videos are in code units, this means that 1 Ms ~ 0.13 code units.

Another video shows the same simulation, however this time the coordinate system is Cartesian instead of polar. In this instance the magnetic field has been given an inclination of 20 degrees with respect to the rotational axis. The draw back of Cartesian geometry when compared to polar, is the greatly reduced resolution around the wind sonic point, to overcome this limitation the wind is held constant to 1.1 stellar radii.

Radio emission form a magnetic massive star. The extended structure of the stellar wind is apparent in high frequency thermal radio emission.

Magnetic Star Planet Interactions

Below are links to videos of MHD simulations of a G-type star, much like our sun, with an orbiting planetary body comparable to that of Jupiter. Both the star and planet are magnetised with dipole fields of 1 G. The winds of both the star and planet are initialised according to Parkers wind model.
